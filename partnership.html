Start date: 1 January 2026

Equity: 15% ordinary Ordinary shares (plain meaning) These are normal company shares. They give you ownership and a vote.
  • 1 share = 1 vote on big decisions.
  • If profit is paid out, you get your share.
  • No special extras or guarantees.
Example: 150 of 1,000 shares = 15% ownership and 15% of any dividend.
Vesting: 24 mo from 1 Jan 2026 • 3‑mo cliff (reverse) Vesting & cliff (simple) Your shares unlock a little each month. If you leave early, the locked part goes back.
  • 24 months total unlocking.
  • 3‑month cliff: nothing unlocks until month 3.
  • After that, they unlock monthly.
Example: Leave in month 8 → 5 months unlocked (months 4–8) = 5/24 of your 15%.
Loan: 0% shareholder loan 0% loan (easy idea) Investor lends money with no interest. Use when needed; pay back when you can.
  • Take money in parts (“drawdowns”).
  • No fixed date to pay back.
  • Unsecured: no assets promised.
  • Subordinated: if things go wrong, other debts get paid first.
Example: Jan: draw €2,000 for notary; Feb: €500 for servers; repay €1,000 in May when cash improves.
Coverage: 24 months (1 Jan 2026 → 31 Dec 2027) Coverage window The support below is meant for this exact period.
  • In: costs dated 2026–2027.
  • Out: costs before 2026 or after 2027 (unless both agree).
Example: Payroll July 2026–June 2027 fits. Payroll Feb 2028 is outside.
Ops guideline: €500/mo (≈€12,000/24m) Monthly ops (rule of thumb) About €500 per month for basics like rent, servers, APIs, domains.
  • Small extra in a month is fine—just agree quickly.
  • Over 24 months this is about €12,000.
Example: Feb costs €700 (annual license) — OK if both agree by email.
DGA salary: 12 months inside 24 months Founder/Director salary (DGA) Covers 12 months of the Founder’s salary during the 24‑month period.
  • Includes employer costs (taxes/benefits).
  • Start month is flexible (once payroll starts).
Example: €3,500 gross + ~25% costs ≈ €4,375/month → 12 months ≈ €52,500.
Milestone: LLM Business Analysis by 30 Jun 2026 Milestone (what “ready” means) A version you can show and sell.
  • Working demo end‑to‑end.
  • Simple pricing & landing page.
  • Short T&Cs/privacy and onboarding steps.
Example: Prospect sees demo → agrees to price → signs basic terms → gets onboarded.
ROFR • Tag‑along • Drag‑along 75% If someone sells shares
  • ROFR: existing owners get first chance to buy.
  • Tag‑along: small owner can join a big owner’s sale at same price.
  • Drag‑along (75%): if 75% agree to sell, everyone sells on same terms.
Example: Buyer offers to buy 60%. Investor (15%) can “tag” along. If 80% agree, they can “drag” the rest.
Dutch law Dutch law (plain) Deals follow Netherlands rules. A Dutch notary records share changes.
  • Bring ID; KVK company papers are often needed.
  • Docs can be in English; notary formalities are in Dutch.
Example: Sign at notary → ownership is official the same day.

  • After that: ROFR, tag‑along, drag‑along at 75% Simple sale rules
    • ROFR: current owners can match a buyer’s offer.

  • If someone leaves early: unlocked vs locked shares If you leave Unlocked (vested) shares stay with you (value at fair price). Locked (unvested) shares go back at tiny “nominal” value.
